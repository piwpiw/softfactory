## Pitch Variations by Audience

### For Investors (VCs, Angels)
- Lead with market size ($5-18B TAM)
- Emphasize unit economics (profitable already)
- Highlight competitive moat (vertical-specific, high switching costs)
- Show founder's personal story (why you care)
- End with ask (funding round, valuation)

### For Customers (Prospects)
- Lead with customer outcome (time saved, cost cut)
- Emphasize ease of use (not technical)
- Show proof (testimonials, case studies)
- Highlight support (onboarding, customer success)
- End with CTA (free trial, demo)

### For Partners (Stripe, Shopify, Google)
- Lead with integration value (how partnership benefits users)
- Emphasize reach (10K users now, 100K in 12 months)
- Highlight mutual benefit (revenue share, co-marketing)
- Show technical compatibility (API design, data models)
- End with partnership framework

### For Press/Media (Journalists, Podcasters)
- Lead with narrative/story (founder journey, customer story)
- Emphasize newsworthiness (new category, market trends)
- Show controversy/opinion (Zapier is overkill, this proves it)
- Highlight expert credibility (domain knowledge, CEO background)
- End with exclusive angle (data, stories, access)

## Tips for Delivery

1. **Personalize.** The best pitch addresses the listener's specific concern. Investor? Emphasize unit economics. Customer? Focus on time savings.

2. **Tell a story.** Data persuades, but stories move. Start with a customer, not a problem.

3. **Use pauses.** Don't rush. Let key points land.

4. **Practice the opening.** The first 30 seconds determine if someone leans in or checks their phone.

5. **Watch for signals.** If someone's eyes glaze over, pivot. Skip the 5-minute version and jump to Q&A.

6. **Be specific.** "6 hours to first booking" beats "fast." "$250/month savings" beats "affordable."

7. **Acknowledge the question.** "Great question..." makes people feel heard.

8. **End with a question.** "What would change if you could launch a service business in a day instead of a quarter?" Engagement > monologue.

9. **Know your numbers.** If you're vague on metrics, you lose credibility.

10. **Believe it.** Conviction is contagious. You need to actually believe this solves a real problem.
